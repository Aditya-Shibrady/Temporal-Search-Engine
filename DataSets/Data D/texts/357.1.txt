Physical findings may include the following: Site of infection marked by a chigger bite. Eschar at the inoculation site (in about 50% of patients with primary infection and 30% of those with recurrent infection). High fever (40-40.5°C [104-105°F]), occurring more than 98% of the time. Tender regional or generalized lymphadenopathy, occurring in 40-97% of cases. Less frequently, ocular pain, wet cough, malaise, and injected conjunctiva. Centrifugal macular rash on the trunk. Enlargement of the spleen, cough, and delirium. Pneumonitis or encephalitis. Central nervous system (CNS), pulmonary, or cardiac involvement. Rarely, acute renal failure, shock, and disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)

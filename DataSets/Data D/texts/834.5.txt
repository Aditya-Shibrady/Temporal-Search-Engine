More than 4,000 people have died in what's been the worst Ebola outbreak the world has ever seen. More than 8,000 people havecontracted the disease in Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone. Those numbers include 401 healthcare workers who have beeninfected; 232 of them have died. Ebola has killed more than half of those infected in the current outbreak. The diseasebegins with flu-like symptoms and in many cases escalates to internal and external bleeding and organ failure.

common symptoms of cholera and the dehydration it causes include: watery, pale-colored diarrhea, often in large amounts nausea and vomiting cramps, particularly in the abdomen and legs irritability, lack of energy, or unusual sleepiness glassy or sunken eyes dry mouth and extreme thirst dry, shriveled skin low urine (pee) output and a lack of tears irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia) and low blood pressure

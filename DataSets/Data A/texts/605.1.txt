Monitoring, characterization and control of chronic, symptomatic malaria infections in rural Zambia through monthly household visits by paid community health workers.Fourteen population clusters of approximately1,000 residents centred around health facilities (HF) in two rural Zambian districts were each provided with three modestly remunerated community health workers (CHWs) conducting active monthly household visits to screenand treat all consenting residents for malaria infection with rapid diagnostic tests (RDT). Both CHWs and HFsalso conducted passive case detection among residents who self-reported for screening and treatment.

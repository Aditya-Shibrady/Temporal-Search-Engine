In 2013, cholera outbreaks were reported in several African countries notably in Angola, Burundi, Congo, Democratic Republic of the Congo and Nigeria. The cases reported from Democratic Republic of the Congo occurred mainly along the borders with Burundi, Congo, Rwanda, Uganda, United Republic of Tanzania and Zambia. A similar situation was observed in 2012, highlighting the pattern of cross-border transmission of V. cholerae.It is notable that the Lake Chad area, which is regularly affected by cholera, did not experience any major outbreak in 2013.

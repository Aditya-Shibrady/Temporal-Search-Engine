During the incubation phase of the disease (6 to 24 weeks), patients may feel unwell with possible nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, anorexia and headaches. Patients may then become jaundiced although low grade fever and loss of appetite may improve. Sometimes HBV infection produces neither jaundice nor obvious symptoms.In areas of Africa and Asia, widespread infection may occur in infancy and childhood. The overall HBsAg carrier rates may be 10 to 15%.

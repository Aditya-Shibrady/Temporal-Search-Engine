Here in Indiana, the Indiana State Department of Health announced Wednesday that it has been monitoring all individuals coming to this state from Liberia, Sierra Leone and Guinea since Oct. 16.Under this "direct active monitoring," state health officials are asking local health departments to take the person's temperature and monitor for Ebolasymptoms twice a day for 21 days after the person returns to the United States. If the person develops symptomsin between these checks, he or she is to immediately notify health officials.

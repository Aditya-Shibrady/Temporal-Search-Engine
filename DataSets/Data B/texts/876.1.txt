Usually, a little over a week after exposure to Ebola virus, people begin having symptoms like fever, chills, muscle pain, sore throat, weakness and general discomfort. In early stages, Ebola can resemble malaria, typhoidfever or bacterial respiratory infections. After five or more days patients often develop signature signs ofan Ebola infection like bumpy red rash on the face, neck, torso and arms. Skin can flake off. Chest pain, bleeding from eyes ears nose mouth.

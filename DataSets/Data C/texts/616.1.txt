The Liberian man diagnosed with Ebola last month just days after arriving in Texas has died following nearly two weeks in a Dallas hospital's isolation room, officials confirmed.Thomas Eric Duncan, believed to be 42, who became the first person diagnosed with the killer virus on American soil during the current pandemic, died early Wednesdayafter a battle that sparked a national debate on screening of visitors from West Africa, which has been ravagedby the disease. His death came a day after officials began treating him with an experimental drug and days after he had taken a dramatic turn for the worse.

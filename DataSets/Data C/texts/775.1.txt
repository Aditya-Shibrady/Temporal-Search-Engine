The current outbreak initiated and reported in Guinea on March 2014 that is also affecting Sierra Leone, Liberia, Nigeria, Senegal, Mali, United States of America and Spain is the most severe and deadly outbreakso far taking into account the cumulative number of cases and deaths. As as illustrated by a tool to trackthe Ebola virus disease outbreak in West Africa, as of November 11th 2014 the World Health Organization (WHO)has reported 14,413 confirmed, probable and suspected cases including 5,177 deaths for a case fatality rate of 36%. Senegal and Nigeria have been declared free of EVD transmission. A forecast of the cumulative numberof Ebola cases in countries with widespread and intense transmission (Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone) helpsto see the expected number of cases in the next weeks assuming the conditions remain similar to the past weeks.
